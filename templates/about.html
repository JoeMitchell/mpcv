Sending a CV is usually the very first step of a job application … except for MPs!

Democracy Club's CV project is getting every candidate for Parliament to share their CV for the voting public.

“To apply, please send your Curriculum Vitae.”

Their future employers (you and me!) can then look at the CVs, and assess their skills and abilities before choosing who is best.

What can I do here?

You can browse the CVs we've already got, and ask your candidates to upload missing ones.

Who's behind this site?

It was first made by Francis Irving. He built a large part of the original TheyWorkForYou, back in the day. The flotilla of people at Democracy Club provide support and infrastructure.

Is there a legal requirement?

It was a recommendation in Sir Christopher Kelly's Committee on Standards in Public Life that all candidates "should publish, at nomination, a register of interests including the existence of other paid jobs and whether they intend to continue to hold them, if elected".

Not quite a CV. We think a full CV will cover some of that, and also give useful background on education, job history and skills. The recommendation was followed up by government uidance.

Candidates send their CV to their party already!

Quite often, yes. This is good evidence that they have a CV. It isn't enough — MPs work for you, not for their party.

What's the long term aim?

Ultimately, we'd like the public to be more involved in the recruitment of MPs in lots of ways. Perhaps we can increase the quality, giving Prime Ministers more choice of high calibre people for senior roles.
